Best Practices for Images in Design

Adding images to your websites is a great way to engage your users and increase the visual appeal of your site.

Important Considerations

But there are a few things to consider when working with images in your designs to ensure they enhance rather than hinder your site.

Responsive Images

The first thing to consider is creating responsive images.

What Are Responsive Images?

Responsive images are images that scale to fit the size of the screen they are being viewed on.

This is important because it ensures that your images look good on all devices, from desktops to mobile phones.

Responsive Image Example

Responsive Image

Try resizing your browser window - the image scales proportionally!

How It Works

Using max-width: 100% and height: auto ensures images scale responsively without distortion.

Image Resolution

Another thing to consider is the resolution for images.

Understanding Resolution

Higher quality images with better resolution have more pixels per inch.

Pixels are small squares that make up an image.

Pixels Per Inch (PPI)

Pixels per inch, or PPI, is the number of pixels in one inch of an image.

The Rule

The higher the PPI, the better the image quality.

More pixels = Higher quality image

High Quality & Optimized

You want to make sure that your images are high quality and look good on all devices.

The Balance

This means that you should use high resolution images that are optimized for the web.

High quality doesn't mean huge file sizes - optimization is key!

Resolution Comparison

❌ Low Resolution

Pixelated and blurry

✓ High Resolution

Sharp and clear

Proper Image Sizing

Another thing to consider is the size of your images and how they fit within the spaces in the layout.

Important Rule

You want to make sure that your images are the right size and are not too large or too small.

Size Impact on Performance

Using large images that are meant to fit in smaller spaces in the design can slow down your website.

Performance Problem

Oversized images make it harder for users to load your site.

You want to make sure that your images are the right size and are optimized for the web.

Image Size Example

Image Placement Principles

When it comes to image placement, you want to think about three key concepts:

Balance

Distribution of visual weight

Hierarchy

Order of viewing importance

Alignment

Placement in relation to other elements

Balance in Design

Balance is the distribution of visual weight in a design.

Creating Harmony

You want to make sure there is a good balance between text and images on the site so it creates a harmonious design.

Balance Example

Balanced Layout

Balance is essential in web design. By evenly distributing visual weight—such as pairing this text with an image—you create a harmonious and pleasing layout.

The text and image have equal visual weight, creating balance.

Hierarchy in Image Placement

Hierarchy is the order in which elements are viewed on a page.

Importance Order

You want to make sure that images that align with important content are placed higher than images that are less important.

Hierarchy Example

Hero Image
(Highest Priority)

Main headline and most important content

Secondary Image

Supporting content with lower priority

Alignment of Images

Alignment is the placement of elements in relation to each other.

Creating Cohesion

You want to make sure that your images are aligned with the text and other elements on your site so that it creates a cohesive design.

Image Accessibility

The last thing to consider is accessibility for images.

Critical for Inclusion

You want to make sure that your images are accessible to all users, including those with visual impairments.

Using Alt Text

Alt Text Is Essential

You should use alt text for your images so that screen readers can read the text to users who are visually impaired.

Example

<img src="cat.jpg" alt="Orange cat sleeping on a blanket">

The alt text describes the image content for users who cannot see it.

Image Best Practices Summary

Responsive

Scale to fit all screen sizes

High Resolution

Good quality with proper PPI

Optimized Size

Right dimensions for layout

Good Placement

Balance, hierarchy, alignment

Accessible

Include descriptive alt text

Create Better Image Experiences

By following these best practices, you create engaging, performant, and accessible image experiences.

Remember

  • Responsive: Works on all devices
  • Quality: High resolution but optimized
  • Performance: Right size for the space
  • Design: Balanced, hierarchical, aligned
  • Inclusive: Accessible to everyone

Great images enhance user experience when implemented thoughtfully!
