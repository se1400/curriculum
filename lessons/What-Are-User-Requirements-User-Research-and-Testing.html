User Research

The systematic study of the people who use your product.

Goal

Measure user needs, behaviors, and pain points.

Net Promoter Score (NPS)

Measures how likely users are to recommend your product to a friend.

Implementation

  • Survey offered at key milestones: 7 days, 30 days, 90 days
  • Scale: 0 to 10
  • Scores of 9 and 10 indicate an active promoter

Exit Interviews

A survey shown to users when they cancel a subscription or delete an account.

Purpose

Provides insight into factors causing user churn, allowing you to address them.

User Testing

The practice of capturing data from users as they interface with your application.

Examples

Beta testing for video games

A/B testing for web development

A/B Testing

Ship a new feature to a randomly selected subset of your user base.

Process

Leverage analytics data to determine if the feature is beneficial.

User Requirements

The stories or rubric that your application needs to follow to inform the development process.

Sources

  • User research
  • Industry standards
  • Stakeholder input

Types of Requirements

Functional Requirements

Dictate how your application should work.

Non-Functional Requirements

Define how your application should behave.

Requirements Are Not Static

User requirements change over time.

Impacting Factors

  • Information from user testing
  • Information from user research
  • Changes in your user base

Understanding the Difference

Understanding the difference between research, testing, and requirements is essential for collecting accurate data.

Result

Deliver the best experience for your end users.
