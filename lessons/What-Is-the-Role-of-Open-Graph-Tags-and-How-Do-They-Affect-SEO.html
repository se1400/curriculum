Open Graph Protocol

The Open Graph protocol enables you to control how your website's content appears across various social media platforms, such as Facebook, LinkedIn, and many more.

By setting these Open Graph properties, you can entice users to want to click and engage with your content.

You set these properties through a collection of meta elements inside your HTML <head> section.

Purpose of OG Tags

  • Control how content appears on social media
  • Improve visual presentation
  • Encourage user engagement
  • Increase click-through rates

OG Title Property

The first important OG property to include would be the title.

For the property attribute, you will need to specify that it is og:title.

The content attribute is where you will write the title you want displayed for social media sites.

OG Title Example
<meta
  property="og:title"
  content="freeCodeCamp.org"
/>

What It Does

Specifies the title that appears when your page is shared on social media platforms

OG Type Property

The next important OG property would be the type.

The type property is used to represent the type of content being shared on social media.

Examples of this content include articles, websites, videos, or music.

OG Type Example
<meta
  property="og:type"
  content="website"
/>

Common Content Types

  • website
  • article
  • video
  • music

OG Image Property

The third important OG property would be the image.

In this example, the Open Graph image is pointing to the freeCodeCamp logo.

All of these images should be high quality with good dimensions and ratios.

OG Image Example
<meta
  property="og:image"
  content="https://cdn.freecodecamp.org/
  platform/universal/fcc_meta_
  1920X1080-indigo.png"
/>

Image Requirements

Most social media platforms will include criteria for image requirements to help you ensure that your content displays well on their site.

Facebook's documentation, for example, provides specific dimension guidelines.

Facebook Image Guidelines

Recommended: At least 1200 x 630 pixels for best display on high resolution devices

Minimum: 600 x 315 pixels to display link page posts with larger images

Best Practices

  • Use high-quality images
  • Follow platform dimension requirements
  • Maintain proper aspect ratios
  • Test on different platforms

OG URL Property

The fourth important OG property would be the url.

This specifies the canonical URL for your page when it's shared on social media.

OG URL Example
<meta
  property="og:url"
  content="https://www.freecodecamp.org"
/>

What It Does

Specifies the canonical URL that represents the page being shared

Additional OG Properties

There are many more OG properties that you can set, like description, audio, video, and locale.

However, the Open Graph url, image, type, and title are the most important ones to include.

Essential Four Properties

  • og:title - Page title
  • og:type - Content type
  • og:image - Preview image
  • og:url - Canonical URL

Optional Properties

  • og:description
  • og:audio
  • og:video
  • og:locale

How OG Properties Affect SEO

When your content is shared on social media, well-crafted OG properties can enhance the appearance for your content in users' feeds.

This can lead to higher click-through rates, which could signal to search engines that your content is relevant and engaging.

SEO Benefits

Direct Impact: Minimal direct effect on search rankings

Indirect Benefits:

  • Enhanced social media appearance
  • Higher click-through rates
  • Increased engagement signals
  • More social traffic to your site
  • Better brand visibility
