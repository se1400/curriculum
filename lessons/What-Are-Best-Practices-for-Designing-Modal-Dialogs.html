Modal Dialogs

A type of pop-up that appears on top of website content.

HTML Implementation

HTML provides a <dialog> element to create modals.

<dialog> <h2>Subscribe to our Newsletter!</h2> <p>Get the latest updates and offers.</p> <button>Subscribe</button> <button>Close</button> </dialog>

Dimmed Background Content

The content behind a modal is usually dimmed.

Purpose

Helps users visually focus on the area you want them to interact with — the modal.

Click Outside to Close

It's always a good idea to allow users to click outside the modal to close it.

User Experience

Provides an intuitive way to dismiss the modal without hunting for a close button.

Call-to-Action (CTA) Buttons

Very prominent buttons on modals designed to prompt specific actions.

Purpose

Should be easily identifiable since you're interrupting the user's flow to prompt a specific action.

<dialog> <h2>Subscribe to our Newsletter!</h2> <p>Get the latest updates and offers.</p> <button class="cta">Subscribe</button> <button class="close">Close</button> </dialog>

Prominent CTA Example

Close Button

Modals should always have a close button.

User Choice

While you want users to click your CTAs, it's important to give them an option to back out and resume what they were doing.

Close Button Example

Newsletter Subscription

Stay updated with our latest news and offers.

Multiple ways to close: X button, Cancel, or click outside

Building a Solid Foundation

Modals have accessibility concerns, such as correctly managing focus on elements.

Best Practices Summary

  • Dim background - Focus user attention
  • Click outside to close - Intuitive dismissal
  • Prominent CTAs - Clear action prompts
  • Close button - Always provide an exit

These practices provide a solid foundation to build accessible modals.
