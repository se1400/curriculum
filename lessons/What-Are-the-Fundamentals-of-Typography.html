What is Typography?

Typography is the art of choosing the right fonts and format to make text visually appealing and easy to read.

"Type" refers to how individual characters are designed and arranged.

Purpose of Typography:

  • Evoke emotions
  • Establish hierarchy
  • Reinforce brand identity

Typeface vs. Font

Typeface

The overall design and style of a set of characters, numbers, and symbols. Like a blueprint for a family of fonts.

Font

A specific variation of a typeface with specific characteristics such as size, weight, style, and width.

Example: Times New Roman Family

Times New Roman Bold

Times New Roman Italic

Serif Typefaces

Serif typefaces have a classical style with small lines at the end of characters.

Characteristics:

  • Small lines (serifs) at the end of strokes
  • Traditional and formal appearance
  • Commonly used for printed materials like books
Typography

Common Serif Typefaces:

Times New Roman, Georgia, Garamond

Sans Serif Typefaces

Sans Serif typefaces have a modern look, without the small lines at the end of characters.

Characteristics:

  • No serifs (clean endpoints)
  • Modern and minimalist appearance
  • Commonly used in digital design
  • Easy to read on screens
Typography

Common Sans Serif Typefaces:

Helvetica, Arial, Roboto

Line Length & Readability

Line length significantly impacts how comfortable text is to read.

Too Narrow

When text is too narrow, readers need to refocus too often, causing eye strain.

Optimal Length

The optimal line length provides comfortable reading without excessive eye movement. This makes the content easier to consume and more enjoyable to read.

Too Wide

When text spans too wide across the screen or page, readers may experience fatigue as their eyes have to travel long distances across each line, making it difficult to find the beginning of the next line.
