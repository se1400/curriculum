Proper Heading Level Structure

What Proper Headings Provide
Proper use of headings creates a visual hierarchy for users to navigate and understand your web page.
Using a logical heading hierarchy allows screen reader users to understand the structure of your content and navigate your content quickly.
Creating appropriate heading text that accurately describes the content that follows helps everyone find the information they need on your site.

Benefits of Proper Heading Structure

Visual Hierarchy
Users can navigate and understand your web page structure
Screen Reader Navigation
Allows users to understand content structure and navigate quickly
Improved SEO
Well-formed headings may improve your site's search engine optimization
Think of It This Way
Headings are the foundation of your site. Without a good foundation, the accessibility of your content will suffer.

How Screen Readers Use Headings

Navigational Structure
Headings, ranging from h1 to h6, create a navigational structure for screen reader users.
Quick Navigation
Screen readers can list all headings on a page, allowing users to jump directly to the sections they need.
Why This Matters
Correctly arranging headings is important for helping these users avoid unnecessary content and finding the information they are looking for quickly.

Reducing Cognitive Load

Who Benefits
People with partial sight or cognitive disabilities need to process information quickly and easily to reduce cognitive load.
Proper Arrangement
While proper arrangement of headings is important, it is also necessary to make heading texts clear and descriptive.

Key Practices for Using Headings

Use headings in a hierarchy that reflects clear organization. For example, the page title should be an h1, major sections should be introduced with h2 headings, subsections with h3, and so on, down to h6.
Don't skip from h1 to h3, or from h2 to h4, and so on.
Use clear and descriptive text that summarizes the content that follows each heading.
Don't use a heading in isolation – some content must follow the heading.
Use appropriate headings where necessary instead of formatting text to look like headings.
Each page should have a single h1 element representing the main topic or title.
