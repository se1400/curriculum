Intellectual Property

Images are considered intellectual property, which means they are protected by copyright regulations.

Copyright most often belongs to the creator of the image.

By default, images are released as "all rights reserved". The creator, or publisher sometimes, holds all copyright for the image.

Default Copyright Status

All Rights Reserved

Unless stated otherwise, all images are automatically protected by copyright and cannot be used without permission.

Using Copyrighted Images

This means that you cannot use copyrighted images in your web page unless you do one of three things.

Each option has specific requirements that must be followed.

Three Ways to Use Copyrighted Images

  • Obtain written permission from the copyright holder
  • Purchase a license from the copyright holder
  • Incorporate the image in a way that falls under fair use

Fair Use

That third point is a bit tricky. Fair use requires that your use of the image is both limited and transformative.

Fair use is a legal doctrine that allows limited use of copyrighted material without permission under specific circumstances.

Fair Use Examples

  • Comment on or review the art
  • Create a parody of the image
  • Educational purposes (in some contexts)
  • News reporting

Important

Fair use is complex and context-dependent. When in doubt, seek legal advice or use properly licensed images.

Permissive Licenses

Some images might be released under a permissive license, like a Creative Commons license, or the BSD license that freeCodeCamp uses.

These images are available for use in your website, but you'll need to read the license to understand the rules you need to follow.

Common Permissive Licenses

  • Creative Commons - Various conditions (attribution, non-commercial, etc.)
  • BSD - Permissive with few restrictions
  • MIT - Very permissive

License Requirements

For example, you might be required to make your website open source, or you may not be permitted to modify the image in any way.

Each license has specific terms you must follow.

Possible License Requirements

  • Attribution required (credit the creator)
  • No modifications allowed
  • Non-commercial use only
  • Share-alike (derivative works under same license)
  • Open source requirement

Always Read the License

Each image's license may have different terms. Read and understand the requirements before using the image.

Public Domain

Finally, some images may be released to the public domain.

An image under the public domain has no copyright attached to it and is free to be used without any restrictions.

Images licensed specifically under the Creative Commons 0 license are considered public domain.

Public Domain Images

Creative Commons 0 (CC0) - Releases works to public domain

  • No copyright restrictions
  • Free to use for any purpose
  • Can be modified freely
  • No attribution required
  • Commercial use allowed

Finding Legal Images

Most search engines will allow you to filter image results by a license.

There are also sites like Pixabay and Unsplash, which offer free-to-use images.

Always be mindful of the copyright and licensing when you use an image in your website.

Free Image Resources

  • Pixabay - Free images and videos
  • Unsplash - High-quality free photos
  • Pexels - Free stock photos and videos
  • Google Images - Use license filters

Search Engine Filters

Most search engines allow you to filter by usage rights to find images you can legally use.

Best Practices

Understanding image licensing is crucial for legal and ethical web development.

Always verify the license before using any image in your projects.

Image Usage Checklist

  • Check the image license before using
  • Read and understand license requirements
  • Provide attribution when required
  • Use free image sites for hassle-free licensing
  • Document your image sources
  • When in doubt, seek permission

Stay Legal

Using images without proper licensing can result in legal issues and copyright infringement claims.
