User-Centered Design

A web development approach that prioritizes the end user — their needs, preferences, and limitations.

Goal

Craft a web page that is:

  • Intuitive
  • Efficient to use
  • Pleasing for users to interact with

Consider Target Demographics

Younger Audience

Leverage flashy, eye-catching designs that grab attention immediately.

Older Audience

Focus on clear and streamlined designs without distractions.

Consider End User Goals

E-Commerce Site

Don't advertise someone else's products on your page — keep focus on your products.

Personal Blog

Include advertisement elements to generate revenue from passive readers.

Measure User Behavior

Leverage Analytics Tools

Use tools like Google Analytics to measure how users engage with your pages.

Analytics can reveal:

  • Areas where users get "stuck" and leave
  • Opportunities to improve interaction flow

Involve Your Users

Provide Feedback Channels

Allow users to share their experiences and pain points with your site.

This captures vital information and enables you to iterate and improve.

Put Users at the Forefront

User-centered design means placing the user at the forefront of your decision making.

Achieve this through:

  • User research
  • Direct feedback
  • Analytics and measurement
  • Iterative improvements
