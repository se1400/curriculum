Dark Mode Best Practices

A feature that changes the default light color scheme to a dark color scheme.

Benefits

  • Reduces eye strain
  • Improves readability in low-light conditions

Designing Effective Dark Mode

Understanding best practices ensures your dark mode feature is effective and user-friendly.

Key Considerations

  • Color saturation
  • Contrast levels
  • Brand identity consistency

Color Saturation

Saturated Colors

Colors that are bright and intense.

Problem: A bright magenta button against a dark gray background can be too intense and cause eye strain.

Best Practice: Use Desaturated Colors

Colors that are less intense, with a lower saturation level, and more comfortable to look at in dark mode.

Contrast: Pure Black vs. Dark Gray

❌ Pure Black + White Text

High contrast can be too harsh on the eyes.

✓ Dark Gray + Light Gray Text

Softer contrast that is easier on the eyes and more comfortable to read.

Dark Mode & Brand Identity

Brand Identity

A set of visual elements that represent a brand:

  • Logo
  • Colors
  • Typography

Implementation Strategy

Ensure dark mode is consistent with your brand's colors and style.

Keep brand icons and buttons at full saturation, while surrounding elements are desaturated.

General Design Principles

Always be mindful of:

  • User experience
  • Contrast levels

Result

By following these best practices, you can create a dark mode feature that is effective and user-friendly.
