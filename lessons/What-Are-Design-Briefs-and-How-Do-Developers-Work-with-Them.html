Design Briefs

When designing new features or applications, a good first step is to create a design brief.

What is a Design Brief?

A document that outlines the objectives, goals, and requirements of a project.

It's a roadmap that guides the design process and ensures the final product meets the client's needs.

Who Creates the Design Brief?

Client-Written

Usually the client writes the design brief and it serves as a working draft.

Designer-Written

Sometimes, the designer writes one and consults with the client to ensure it meets their needs.

Key Elements of a Design Brief

Several key elements should be included:

  • Overview of project and business
  • Goals and objectives
  • Target audience
  • Competition analysis
  • Project scope

Element 1: Project & Business Overview

Should include:

  • Company's details
  • Company mission
  • Company values
  • Unique selling points
  • Products or services

Element 2: Goals & Objectives

Document the goals and objectives for the project:

  • The purpose of the project
  • The desired outcomes

Examples of Goals

  • Increasing traffic to a site
  • Increasing monthly page visits by X percent

Element 3: Target Audience

Include information about the target audience:

  • Target demographics
  • Audience interests
  • Audience needs

Element 4: Competition Analysis

Include information about the competition and how the project will differentiate itself from competitors.

Element 5: Project Scope

Should include:

  • Deliverables - List of all items to be produced (mockups, final designs)
  • Timeline - When items will be delivered
  • Budget - Financial constraints

Why Define Scope Clearly?

Problem Without Clear Scope

Things can get out of hand and go over budget.

Solution

Be as detailed as possible about what is expected to be delivered and by when.

Timescale & Budget Challenges

One of the challenging aspects of project design is timescale and budget.

Importance

Be realistic about what can be achieved within the given timeframe and budget.

Having a design brief that outlines these constraints is important.

Design Work Begins

The design brief should be reviewed and approved by all stakeholders before the project begins.

Only then can designers get started with their work.

The Developer's Role

Take the designs, understand the project requirements, and turn them into a working product.

This Involves:

  • Writing code
  • Testing the application
  • Debugging to ensure it meets requirements outlined in the design brief

Working in Teams

Developers often work in teams where work is split between multiple people.

Why Developers Need to Understand the Brief

While you might not be involved in the design and initial decision-making process as a developer, it's still important to:

  • Understand the design brief
  • Know how it will impact your work

Key Takeaways

  • Design brief - Document outlining objectives, goals, and requirements
  • Key elements - Overview, goals, audience, competition, scope
  • Project scope - Critical for staying on track and within budget
  • Review process - Must be approved by stakeholders before work begins
  • Developer role - Turn designs into working product through code, testing, debugging
  • Understanding matters - Developers must understand the brief to work effectively
