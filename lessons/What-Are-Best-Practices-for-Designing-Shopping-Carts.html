Shopping Cart Design

The shopping cart is a crucial part of the e-commerce experience.

Impact of Design

Good design: Makes shopping more enjoyable and increases sales

Poor design: Leads to abandoned carts and lost sales

Best Practice: Always Visible

Shopping cart should be visible at all times.

Standard Placement

Most designs display the cart in the upper right-hand corner of the page.

Best Practice: Update Quantity

Provide a clear way for users to update item quantities.

Implementation

Include a quantity input field next to each item in the cart.

Users can easily update by changing the number in the field.

Best Practice: Easy Removal

Provide a "Remove" button next to each item in the cart.

Importance

Allows users to easily remove items from their cart.

Problem: Making it difficult to remove items leads to frustration and abandoned carts.

Best Practice: Recognizable Icon

The icon should be easily recognizable for all users.

Avoid

Icons that are too abstract or difficult to understand.

Best Practice: Prominent Total

Users should easily find the total cost of all items in the cart.

Implementation

Display the total prominently on the page — users shouldn't have to search for it.

Best Practice: Clear Call-to-Action

Provide a clear call-to-action button to proceed to checkout.

Button Design

  • Prominently displayed — easy to find
  • Most prominent button on the page
  • Use the brand's primary color to stand out
  • Avoid too many buttons — prevents confusion

Best Practices Summary

  • Always visible - Cart in upper right corner with item count
  • Update quantity - Input field next to each item
  • Easy removal - "Remove" button for each item
  • Recognizable icon - Use familiar cart/bag symbols
  • Prominent total - Display cost clearly and prominently
  • Clear CTA - Checkout button in brand color, easy to find
